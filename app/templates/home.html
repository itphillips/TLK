The Linguist's Kitchen is a web-based app, designed to help guide students in introductory linguistics courses in dissecting their own language(s). The Linguist's Kitchen provides a workspace where you can transcribe language samples recorded at home or in the community and the tools to work through three levels of guided syntactic analysis.

The first level guides the user through identifying parts of speech (noun, verb, adjective, pronoun, etc.)

The second level guides the user through identifying phrase structure (noun phrase, verb phrase, adjectival phrase, etc.)

The third level guides the user through identifying grammatical function (grammatical subject, grammatical direct object, etc.)

Once you have tagged some sentences with these three levels of info, the app will generate a phrase structure (PS) grammar based on the input.

A PS grammar is a list of transformation rules like:
S = NP VP
NP = (Det) N
VP = V (NP)
etc.
